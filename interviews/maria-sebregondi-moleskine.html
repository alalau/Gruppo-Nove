  • Maria Sebregondi

    Maria Sebregondi co-founded Moleskine after noticing a new category of “tech nomads” while she was traveling with 3 friends who were inspired by culture and design. In 1995, Maria brought the Moleskine brand to Milan. Her story of transforming this simple, historical notebook into a cultural icon of imagination, travel and personal identity, and attaching those values to a company is an invaluable lesson in branding. Currently she is responsible for the Moleskine brand worldwide as the vice president of brand equity and communications.

  • “We never start thinking about an object. We always ask, “Who is the person that we want to inspire, to address, to profit?"”

  • “Brands are characterized by intangible values. So there is something which is going beyond the single product or the single service - which is something related with intangible, emotional, cognitive experiences.”

This interview was conducted by Taylor Ward and Roxanne Henschke
